Pancakes with syrup and butter

A simple recipe for scotch/american style pancakes, fluffy and unsweetened, with the option of additional syrup and butter (or any sweetening of your choice!). The recipe follows a 1:1:1 ratio by weight of ingridents and should make atleast 4 pancakes. On average a large egg out of its shell is roughly 60g. (This recipe can be altered to create a bigger batch of batter)

Ingredients

  • 60g of egg
  • 60g of flour
  • 60g of milk

Recipe

  1. Seperate egg whites and yolk, whisk the whites till they form soft peaks.
  2. Add the egg yolks to the flour. Incorporate milk to the flour slowly creating a even batter with a few lumps (don't worry these lumps create the fluffyness of the pancakes!)
  3. Slowly but steadly incorporate the whisked egg whites to the batter, gently folding the mixture into itself.
  4. Use a ladle to scoop the batter, place on to a hot pan, pregreaced with butter.
  5. Keep an eye on the pancakes, flip once bubbles start to pop and a brown crust has formed. Remove from heat once both sides have been cooked.
  6. Stack pancakes high on a plate and serve with butter and maple syrup. (or any sweet toppings of your choice)
